2.4 2.3.3 The Minus-1 Trick

For a homogeneous system \(A\mathbf{x} = 0\) in RREF: 1. Extend \(A\) to an \(n \times n\) matrix \(\tilde{A}\) by adding rows with a single −1 where pivots are missing.
2. The columns of \(\tilde{A}\) containing −1 entries form a basis for the solution space (kernel/null space).

Example (same as above): \[ \tilde{A} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 3 & 0 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & 9 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 1 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \] The columns with −1 yield the same solution vectors as before.
