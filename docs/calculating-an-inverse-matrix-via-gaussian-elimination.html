2.5 Calculating an Inverse Matrix via Gaussian Elimination

To find \(A^{-1}\) for \(A \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}\): \[ [A | I_n] \;\Rightarrow\; [I_n | A^{-1}] \] using Gaussian elimination.

2.5.1 Example:

\[ A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 2 & 0 \\ 1 & 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 2 & 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \]

After elimination: \[ A^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 2 & -2 & 2 \\ 1 & -1 & 2 & -2 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 0 & -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \]

Verification: \[ A A^{-1} = I_4. \]
